We are an XR design collective of artists, architects, and technologists creating immersive experiences at the intersection of research, space, and emerging technology. Our work spans exhibitions, digital environments, public installations, and experimental interfaces.

OUR TEAM

Shruti Sharma

Shruti Sharma received her doctorate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Princeton University and was advised in experiential design by Edwin Schlossberg. She contextualized the abstraction of quantum phenomena in her doctorate through contemporary art and poetry which was exhibited in New York and Venice. In 2021, she co-founded the Princeton University media laboratory, Museumverse, at the intersection of art, curation, and research, for which she remains a Principal Investigator. Her background is in condensed matter physics through her masters degree at the University of Cambridge as a Gates-Cambridge scholar and at MIT in her bachelors degree in Materials Science & Engineering (concentration in French Language & Literature).

Yuchen Shi

Yuchen Shi is a multidisciplinary artist and designer based in Princeton, New Jersey. She specializes in painting and graphic design, while also working across installation, moving image, and interactive media. Currently pursuing an A.B. in Practice of Art at Princeton University, with minors in Computer Science and Architecture & Engineering, her practice explores how visual systems shape perception, communication, and human connection. Drawing inspiration from architecture, scientific inquiry, and everyday interactions, she creates works that translate complex ideas into accessible, playful, and participatory experiences. Whether through paintings, printed matter, exhibitions, or digital environments, Shi approaches design as both a tool for storytelling and a medium for building relationships between people and ideas.

Robin Resch

Robin Resch is a visual artist based in Princeton, New Jersey, known for natural portraiture and fine-art photography. Her landscape work explores the power of nature and the tension between ephemerality and continuity, privileging emotional experience over literal representation. Her photographs are held in private collections in the United States and Europe, and her career highlights include serving as the exclusive campaign photographer for First Lady Michelle Obama (2012), an Artist in Residence with the Arts Council of Princeton and Princeton University’s Humanities Council (2020–21), commissioned cover artist for Princeton University Reunions (2023), and the two-person exhibition Being Present at the Arts Council of Princeton (2025).

Devin Greenwood

Devin Greenwood is an American audio/visual artist and composer of contemporary classical music based in New York City. Inspired by wave phenomena such as weather patterns and collective behavior, his music centers around a compositional practice he developed with Gideon Crevoshay called antenati—a name which refers to the emergence of spontaneous musical structures from a canon of tension-resolution patterns performed by the composer. In his scholarly work, A New Phenomenology of Meaning in Music, Greenwood highlights the underlying patterns generated by the practice, linking the emergence of musical structures to the oscillatory nature of thought and subjective experience. His visual work is focused on obsolete tech such as oscilloscopes, video synthesizers, and 1980s game consoles. He is the co-founder/owner of the Brooklyn recording studio The Honey Jar, and in 2024, he was awarded a doctoral fellowship at Princeton University, where he is pursuing a PhD in music cognition and composition.
